Transform the person in the reference photo into a HAND-DRAWN minimalist line-art sketch — the kind of casual avatar illustration people use as social media profile pictures.

STYLE:
- Pure black brush-pen ink on clean white paper
- Medium-thick lines with natural slight wobble and variation — NOT perfectly straight, NOT uniform thickness
- The final image must look like a human illustrator's 3-minute quick sketch, NOT AI-generated

DRAWING RULES — use the absolute minimum strokes:
- Entire body outer silhouette: ONE flowing contour line, no more
- Hair: 3-5 strokes for the overall shape, 1-2 loose interior lines for volume. NO individual strands
- Face: EXACTLY 4-6 marks — two small dots or curves for eyes, one tiny nose stroke, one short mouth line. NO jaw outline, NO ear detail, NO eyebrows
- Clothing: outer silhouette only, 2-4 interior lines for collar or major fold. Ignore seams, textures, minor wrinkles
- Hands: 2-3 abstract lines suggesting shape. Skip individual fingers unless the hand is the main focus
- If any body part has more than 15 strokes, you are overdrawing — stop and simplify

CONSTRAINTS:
- 1:1 square image
- Pure black ink on pure white background — NO color, NO gray, NO shading, NO hatching, NO gradient
- Solid black fill ONLY where the person's clothing is visibly dark or black
- Total strokes across the whole figure: under 50

The result should feel effortless, stylish, and minimal — a gestural sketch, not a detailed portrait.
